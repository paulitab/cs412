About Picasso

Pablo Picasso (1881–1973) was a Spanish painter, sculptor, printmaker, ceramicist, and stage designer who spent most of his adult life in France. He is regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, known for co-founding the Cubist movement, the invention of constructed sculpture, and the co-invention of collage.

Born in Málaga, Spain, Picasso demonstrated extraordinary artistic talent at a young age, painting in a realistic style through his childhood and adolescence. Throughout his career, he experimented with various techniques, including Surrealism and Symbolism. Some of his most famous works include "Guernica," a powerful anti-war painting, and "Les Demoiselles d'Avignon," which marked a turning point in modern art.

His immense body of work, creativity, and innovation made him a pioneering figure in modern art, with influence extending well beyond painting. His prolific career produced over 20,000 works of art in different mediums.
